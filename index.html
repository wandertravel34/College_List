Reality-check reminders

  • UCSD and UF moved to Wildcard. Both are aspirational reaches for a 3.7 UW / 1200 SAT profile. Keeping them visible without pretending they're realistic.
  • CU Boulder is the strongest must-have — legitimate target and right in the fairway.
  • "Biz: Diff" schools have a second admissions hurdle beyond the university admit. Plan on maintaining a strong college GPA freshman year to clear it.
  • Kelley (Indiana) is the sneakiest one on the list — Indiana's overall admit rate is 85% but Kelley direct-admit is one of the toughest undergrad business admits in the country. Worth flagging.
  • Merit aid at wildcards matters: DU, USD, and Chapman all publish merit tiers tied to GPA/SAT. Run their net-price calculators once his SAT is in.
  • Once his SAT score is in: a 1300+ shifts several stretches (Wisconsin, Clemson) toward target; an 1100 pushes UCs further out and moves a few targets into safety.

Regional exchange programs — parent reference

WUE (Western Undergraduate Exchange) lets California students pay ~150% of in-state tuition (usually well below full OOS rates) at participating public universities in these states: AZ, CO, ID, MT, ND, NM, NV, OR, SD, UT, WA, WY, HI. Not all campuses or majors participate, and business programs are sometimes excluded — verify per school before applying.

Schools on this list where WUE is a meaningful cost lever: U Arizona (full participation), NAU, Colorado Mesa, Metro State Denver, Western Colorado, Adams State, U Utah (partial), CU Boulder (partial), Boise State, U Hawaii. WUE brings OOS costs at some of these from $50-60K down to $25-40K.

Other regional exchanges to know:

  • Good Neighbor programs at specific state schools (e.g., Utah State's Good Neighbor rate) — check per school
  • Legacy / alumni tuition breaks if either parent attended a school — ask directly
  • Merit aid at privates (DU, USD, Chapman, Creighton, Pepperdine) can bring net cost below sticker; run each school's net price calculator once SAT is in
  • SEC schools with OOS scholarships — Alabama, Ole Miss, Mississippi State, and Arkansas are known for automatic OOS merit tied to GPA/SAT bands. Alabama's Presidential/Foundation scholarships and Ole Miss's Academic Excellence Awards are worth researching.

Action item for parents: once his SAT score is in, spend an evening running the net price calculator on each Wildcard school and each private on the list. Real net cost can differ from sticker by $20-40K.
